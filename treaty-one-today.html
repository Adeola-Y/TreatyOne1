Treaty One

We acknowledge being on Treaty One territory and commit to moving forward in partnership with Indigenous communities towards reconciliation. We recognize that we are gathered on Treaty 1 Territory and that Manitoba is located on the Treaty Territories and ancestral lands of the Anishinaabeg, Anishininewuk, Dakota Oyate, Denesuline, and Nehethowuk Nations.

Modern Day Treaties

Modern treaties, also known as Comprehensive Land Claims Agreements, are concluded over long periods of time and contain a high level of detail. Are usually between Indigenous peoples, the federal and provincial Crown and in some cases, a territory.These treaties enable Indigenous peoples to rebuild their communities and nations on their own terms. A great example of this would be the formerly part of Canadian Forces Base in Winnipeg.

Kapyong Barracks:

The former Military base Kenaston Boulevard closed in 2004, when its occupants moved to Canadian Forces Base Shilo, near Brandon. After determining that the Canadian Armed Forces no longer had an operational need for the property, the Department of National Defence (DND) received approval to sell the property to the Canada Lands Company CLC Limited in 2007. First Nations seeked to enforce their rights (as treaty land entitlements) under modern agreements with Canada went to court on the fact that Canada never fulfilled the requirements stated in their treaty. As the Federal Court of Appeal found, Canada “broke the solemn promise it made”.

Who Bought The Land:

On August 30, 2019, Canada and the Treaty One First Nations signed a Comprehensive Settlement Agreement, based on the shared understandings outlined in the April 2018 Agreement in Principle. The agreement was to sell 68% of the Kapyong Barracks land to the Treaty One First Nations. The remaining 32% of the property was to be sold to the Canada Lands Company CLC Limited for redevelopment. the demolition contract was also under the Federal Government Set‑Aside for Aboriginal Business, in accordance with the Procurement Strategy for Aboriginal Business (PSAB).

Future Plans

DND is demolishing on-site infrastructure at the Kapyong Barracks. The first phase of demolition was completed in May 2019, and the remaining demolition work was completed in the fall of 2021. The final master plan shows the site could have up to three thousand new residences and 1.2 million square feet of commercial space. The 168-acre site will also have space for sports and recreation facilities, community spaces and an administration centre for Treaty 1 First Nations. In 2021, the Treaty One Nations named the property Naawi-Oodena, which means “centre of the heart and community” in the Anishinaabe language. The concept of an urban reserve allows First Nations to use land within the city to develop for commercial purposes and generate revenue for their community.
